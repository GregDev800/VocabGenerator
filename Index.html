When should we use Present Simple tense?

1. Describing repeatable activities:

* I work on Sundays.

2. Describing feelings, conditions, traits, long-term situations:

* I work as an architect.
* She is asleep.
* The soup smells nice.

3. Describing nature laws, genuine phenomenons:

* Water boils in 100 degrees centigrade.

When should we use Present Continuous tense?

1. Describing actual activities:

* I'm cooking right now.

2. Describing temporary activities:

* I'm working as a waitress this summer.

3. Describing specific plans for the nearest future:

* I'm going out tomorrow evening.

4. Describing changeable conditions and situations:

* It's getting dark and chilly.

5. Describing frequent and burdensome events (always + continually):

* My children are continually disrupting me.

When should we use Present Perfect tense?

1. Describing results of actions from the past:

* The mechanic has finally mended my car so I am able to visit you now.

2. Describing an action/condition started in the past and persisting until now:

* I have lived in this city since I was 8 yo.
* How long have you known Karen?

3. Describing an unfinished period of time yet:

* How many flats have you and your partner browsed (so far) this week?

4. Describing experiences and achievements of alive people:

* Have you ever been to Japan?
* My childhood friend has visited almost every country in Europe.

When should we use Future Simple tense?

1. Describing spontaneous decisions, undertaken in the moment of speaking:

* Somebody is ringing at the door. I will open it.

2. Describing own assumptions:

* I expect Timothy will say sorry to me some day.

3. Expressing promises and threats:

* I will prepare your favourite snacks next time you visit me.

4. Saying about dreams and general plans for the future:

* I will attend the best uni at my city once I've finished my high school.

5. Describing activities, which in our opinion, will repeat in the future:

* My parents will always help me when I am in trouble.

6. Expressing polite requests:

* Will you do the laundry today, please?

When should we use Past Simple tense?

1. Describing actions, conditions, situations from the past not having any reference to the present:

* I learnt English many years ago.

2. Describing habits from the past:

* My dad had a cup of coffee and read every morning.

3. Describing an event from the past without pointing on the occurrence time:

* Dinosaurs went extinct millions of years ago.

4. Asking about a time/date of actions occurred in the past:

* What time did your kids wake up for school?

5. Describing a sequence of events that happened one after another without a significant time gap:

* He opened the door, came in and gave a kiss for goodbye.

When should we use Past Continuous tense?

1. Referring to a past action without providing any information about its completion:

* I was doing that, it was a sheer delight!

2. Describing an activity taking place continually in the past (+ its duration):

* You weren’t talking to each other the whole party, could I know why?

3. While describing an action in a specific moment in the past or whilst asking about that action:

* It was raining heavily three hours ago.

4. While taking about at least TWO actions, which were taking place in the same time:

* Tony was driving while Katie was scrolling her smartphone.

5. Describing an event that repeated in the past and was irritating:

* Our neighbour was always complaining about our 4 am morning routine (singing outloud).

6. When we talk about an activity from the past, that was interrupted by the 2nd, shorter one:

* I was sleeping when you rang me.
